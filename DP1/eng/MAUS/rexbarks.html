  • Chapter 4 - Dependent Clauses
    • Defn
      • Phrase: groups of words w/o a subject and verb; single part of speech
      • Clause: groups of words w/ a subject and verb
      • ex. When did Rex Bark?
        • Yesterday - an adverb
        • In the afternoon - an adverb phrase
        • When the mailman came - an adverb clause
    • Adverb Clauses
      • subordinating
        • introduces an adverb clause and joins it to a main clause
      • eliiptical clause
        • w/ some words left out
        • "THAN" IS ALWAYS A CONJUNCTION!
    • Adjective Clauses
      • dependent clauses
        • adj
        • adv
        • n
      • direction:
        • a. find the main clause. Diagram it.
        • b. hang the relative pronoun from the noun or pronoun it refers to.
        • c. find the adjective clause. Diagram it with reference to the relative pronoun.
        • d. substitute the noun or pronoun at the top of the dotted line for the relative pronoun. Does it make sense?
      • 3 Cases for who
        • nominative -- who
        • possessive -- whose
        • objective -- whom
      • relative pronouns
        • WHO family -- people
        • WHICH -- things
        • THAT --poeple / things
      • antecendent
        • ex: Harry rode his bike, then the antecedent of his is harry
      • Restrictive / Non restrictive clauses
        • Restrictive when the adj clause is necessary to identify the antecedent of the relative pronoun
